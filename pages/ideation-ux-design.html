// Reflections

Critical Commentaries

"What role does a good idea play in a successful digital artefact?"

A strong idea is the foundation of any successful digital product. It defines the user's experience, functionality, and overall impact. Without a compelling core concept, even technically flawless execution fails to engage the people it's built for.

"Why does knowledge of ideation techniques matter?"

Established ideation techniques — like brainstorming, mind mapping, and SCAMPER — offer structured approaches to creative problem-solving. They make the generation of original ideas repeatable and teachable, rather than a matter of luck or inspiration.

"What do you understand by 'the process is more important than the product'?"

This principle emphasises that the skills, habits, and thinking developed during creation matter more than the finished output. Each iteration teaches more than the final result. The process builds the person; the product is merely its evidence.

"What does 'generating ideas as a professional habit' mean?"

Professional idea generation is a mindset of constant curiosity and creative flexibility — always looking for innovative solutions rather than defaulting to the familiar. It means cultivating openness to unexpected directions and making ideation a daily discipline, not an event.

FITNESS — Your Gym Companion

FITNESS is an all-in-one gym and workout app designed for fitness enthusiasts aged 18–40. It solves three core problems: finding the right workout routine, managing gym memberships, and tracking real progress over time.

Custom Workout Routines — tailored to the user's fitness goals and experience level

Gym Membership Management — apply and manage memberships directly in-app

Progress Tracking — real-time analytics and goal setting with visual displays

Expert Fitness Tips — professional guidance accessible at every stage

// User Research

Understanding the User

Who They Are

Fitness enthusiasts — both beginners and experienced individuals — who want structure and guidance in their fitness routine, aged 18–40.

Where They Face the Problem

At home, the gym, office, school, and university — wherever managing workouts, memberships, and nutrition becomes overwhelming without the right tools.

How Often

Daily or weekly — fitness challenges are recurring, not one-off. The app needs to be a consistent companion rather than a periodic reference.

The Core Problem

Managing workouts, gym memberships, and nutrition simultaneously is fragmented across multiple apps and platforms — users want one solution.

Why It Matters

Physical health and fitness directly impact wellbeing. Friction in accessing fitness tools creates genuine barriers — this app removes those barriers.

Research Method

Audience research conducted via Google Forms — collecting real feedback from the target group about their pain points and preferences before any design began.

// Market Analysis

Competitor Research

MyFitnessPal

Straightforward and easy to navigate. Quick access to key functions like calorie tracking and exercise recording via a clean dashboard.

Large food database, device/app integration, detailed meal logging, and comprehensive progress monitoring.

Users report occasional accuracy issues in the food database and cluttered experience from some premium-locked features.

Nike Training Club

Eye-catching, contemporary layout — simple navigation and clean visual hierarchy that feels premium and polished.

High-quality workouts from certified trainers, ranging from beginner to intermediate — personalised suggestions and progress tracking.

Some users report technical issues and app crashes — reliability concerns that FITNESS aims to avoid from the start.

FITNESS differentiates itself by combining workout routines, gym membership management, and progress tracking in a single seamless experience — addressing the fragmentation that both competitors leave unsolved.

— SA2 Market Research, M.Rashid
// User Testing

Real Feedback

✓ Positive

Overall Experience

Users found FITNESS easy to use and intuitive. Navigation through features was smooth and the design felt user-friendly from the first interaction.

✓ Positive

Workout Routines

Exercise regimens were praised for accommodating varying fitness levels, with clear directions and good variety — users appreciated the thoroughness of each routine.

✓ Positive

Progress Tracking

The visual progress displays were well-liked — users found the tracking tool made it easy and motivating to monitor their fitness improvements over time.

→ Suggestion

Onboarding Tutorial

New users suggested adding a guided tutorial to help them discover all features quickly — particularly useful for less tech-savvy fitness users.

→ Suggestion

Membership Data

One user noted some gym information was out of date — feedback led to the idea of a user-submitted update system to keep gym data accurate in real time.

→ Bug Found

Nutrition Section Crash

One tester experienced crashes accessing the nutrition guide — a key technical issue flagged for resolution before any real-world launch of the app.

// Reflection

Key Learnings

01

The process builds the designer, not the product

The FairyCup Chandelier and the FITNESS prototype taught the same lesson from opposite ends — the creative decisions, constraints, and iterations in between matter far more than the finished artefact.

02

User research before design, always

Starting with Google Forms feedback before opening Figma changed the quality of every decision that followed. The user's problem — not the designer's assumption — should drive every feature.

03

Market saturation demands a sharp differentiator

The fitness app space is crowded. Analysing MyFitnessPal and Nike Training Club showed that FITNESS's real opportunity was solving the fragmentation problem — one app, three core needs.

04

User testing reveals what the designer can't see

The nutrition section crash was invisible to me during design. Real testing with real users found it instantly. No prototype is finished until it's been tested by someone who didn't build it.

05

Ideation is a professional skill, not a personality trait

Across all four live challenge sessions, structured ideation techniques produced better outputs than free-form brainstorming alone. Creativity is a practice — and a learnable one at that.
