The Brief

Prime Marketing & Construction needed a complete digital identity to promote their new development, Prime Arcade — attracting potential buyers and investors through a cohesive brand presence across social media, promotional materials, and digital advertising. The challenge: build something that looks professional, trustworthy, and compelling from zero.

Build a complete digital brand identity for the Prime Arcade launch

Potential buyers, investors, and the wider community interested in the development

Positive client feedback validating the campaign's professional execution and brand impact

The Creative Industry Challenge is the module where university work meets real professional life. Instead of a hypothetical brief, Team 11 worked with an actual client — Prime Marketing & Construction — to deliver real-world outputs that were actually used to promote their project.

The stakes were higher than any coursework assignment. The client's brand, reputation, and commercial goals were on the line. Every design decision had to balance creative quality against client expectations, project requirements, and team coordination.

Prime Arcade is a real development project — our goal was to make it look like it had a seasoned marketing team behind it. The campaign had to communicate three things clearly: location, facilities, and investment potential.

"This project taught me how to balance creativity with client expectations, and how to manage responsibilities in a real-world, team-driven setting. The client's positive feedback validated our work."

— Collaboration Statement, M.Rashid

// Reflection

Key Learnings

01

Real clients change everything

Working with Prime Marketing & Construction wasn't like a coursework brief. The client had real stakes, real preferences, and real expectations. Every design had to work for them — not just look good in a portfolio. That constraint produced better work.

02

Visual consistency is the foundation of brand trust

Making every poster, post, and template feel like they came from the same brand required discipline — not just creativity. Consistent colour, typography, and messaging tone is what makes a company look professional rather than cobbled together.

03

Design must serve the brief, not the designer

Highlighting Prime Arcade's location, facilities, and investment potential wasn't always the most visually exciting direction — but it was the right one for the client's goals. Good design serves the audience, not the ego of the person making it.

04

Cross-team alignment is a creative skill

Making sure the website, posters, and video all felt like one campaign required active communication — not just doing my part and moving on. Brand alignment across teams is as much a design skill as anything done in Canva.

05

Client validation is the ultimate grade

The client's positive feedback mattered more than any mark. It confirmed that the work was genuinely useful, professional, and effective — not just academically correct. That distinction is what makes industry experience different from coursework.
