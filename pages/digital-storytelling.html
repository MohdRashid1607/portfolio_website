// Overview

About This Module

Digital Storytelling isn't just about writing — it's about choosing the right medium to carry a story. This module challenged me to tell stories across radically different formats: interactive games, stop-motion animation, short films, photography, and non-linear branching narratives.

From asking the fundamental question — "What qualifies as a story?" — to building a fully playable Twine adventure with 25 interconnected passages, this module reshaped how I think about narrative and user experience.

"Stories have a profound impact on our lives. They guide us by providing examples to follow, inform us about the world, inspire our passions, and teach us lessons about resilience, teamwork, and perseverance."

— M.Rashid, Reflective Commentary

The module explored critical theory first — how stories affect audiences by guiding, informing, inspiring, teaching, warning, and moving them — before applying those ideas to real digital outputs across multiple platforms.

A standout realisation: computing and storytelling are not separate disciplines. The Bitsy game, the Twine adventure, the stop-motion film — all proved that code can be a medium for emotion, not just function.

The capstone was a collaborative short film — "Welcome to Bath Spa University, RAK" — a real advertisement requiring script development, filming, and production planning, which received positive feedback from viewers upon completion.

// Theory

What Qualifies as a "Story"?

Narrative Structure

A story follows a structured format — beginning, middle, and end — giving audiences a sense of journey and resolution that feels complete.

Characters

Stories feature characters who grow, change, or face challenges. Their journeys create connection and allow audiences to see reflections of themselves.

Conflict & Tension

Most stories involve conflict — internal, external, or both — that drives the plot forward and keeps audiences emotionally invested.

Theme

Stories explore moral, philosophical, or social themes — giving audiences ideas and values to carry beyond the narrative itself.

Emotion & Engagement

Good stories evoke genuine emotions — joy, sadness, excitement, fear, empathy. Emotional engagement is what separates a story from mere information.

Medium of Communication

Stories exist across written word, visual art, film, music, and interactive games. The medium shapes the experience just as much as the narrative.

// Reflection

Key Learnings

01

Computing is a medium for emotion, not just function

The Bitsy game and Twine adventure proved that code can carry narrative weight. Writing branching logic and designing rooms isn't just technical — it's storytelling with a different kind of pen.

02

The medium shapes the story as much as the content does

The same concept told through stop-motion, a video game, or a short film creates completely different experiences. Choosing the right medium is a creative decision, not an afterthought.

03

Non-linear narratives demand deeper design thinking

Building a Twine game with 25 passages required planning every branch, every consequence, and every dead end. The most demanding design challenge of the module — and the most rewarding.

04

Collaboration multiplies creative output

The BathSpa RAK advertisement required real teamwork — script writing, filming logistics, and production coordination. The final product was far stronger than anything I could have built alone.

05

Adaptability across platforms is a core creative skill

Navigating Bitsy, Twine, Photoshop, stop-motion rigs, and video editing simultaneously demanded platform adaptability — a skill that carries directly into professional development work.
