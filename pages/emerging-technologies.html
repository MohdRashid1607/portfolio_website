// Experiment 1

"The Race Against Time" — Olympic Data Story

This project explores the evolution of the Olympics from 1900 to 2024 through five interactive visualisations — going beyond medal counts to reveal deeper patterns in gender representation, geopolitical power, and individual athlete dominance.

Built using Flourish Studio for its intuitive storytelling features and professional polish, each chart type was carefully selected to fit the specific data story it needed to tell. The dataset spans over 120 years of Olympic history, cleaned and filtered using Excel.

"Good data visualisation reveals hidden stories — such as athlete medal patterns or geopolitical influence on Olympic performance. Data becomes narrative in the digital age."

— Critical Reflection, M.Rashid

✓ For 5 Arguments in Favour
💰 Economic Growth

Global eSports valued at $1.8B in 2023, projected to surpass $3B by 2026 — driven by sponsorships, media rights, and merchandise.

💼 Career Opportunities

Thousands of jobs in coaching, event management, broadcasting, and software development. Universities now offer eSports scholarships and degrees.

🤖 Technological Advancement

AI analytics, cloud gaming, and blockchain are driving innovation. eSports is a proving ground for emerging technologies at scale.

🌍 Social Inclusivity

Unlike physical sports, eSports provides opportunities regardless of physical ability, background, or location — a genuinely inclusive competitive arena.

🎓 Educational Integration

Institutions integrating eSports develop transferable skills — strategic thinking, teamwork, problem-solving, and communication under pressure.

✗ Against 5 Arguments Against
🏥 Health Concerns

Professional gamers train 10–12 hours daily — leading to eye strain, back pain, carpal tunnel syndrome, and documented mental health issues.

⚖️ Integrity & Regulation

Unlike FIFA or NBA-governed sports, eSports lacks a universal regulatory body — raising concerns over match-fixing, doping, and inconsistent standards.

🏈 Impact on Traditional Sports

Younger audiences shifting to digital gaming — major leagues like NFL and NBA are losing viewership and exploring eSports partnerships defensively.

😤 Toxicity & Harassment

Cyberbullying and harassment are prevalent in competitive gaming communities — particularly affecting female players and minority groups at scale.

🌱 Environmental Impact

Significant energy consumption from servers, data centres, and high-performance rigs raises serious sustainability and carbon footprint concerns.

3 Global Case Studies

🇰🇷

South Korea — The Birthplace of eSports

With government support from KeSPA, South Korea built professional leagues, specialised training facilities, and broadcast infrastructure that turned eSports into a legitimate national institution.

// Reflection

Key Learnings

01

Data storytelling is a distinct skill from data analysis

Choosing the right chart type for each story — a radar for an individual athlete, a chord diagram for relationships, a bar race for rankings over time — required design thinking, not just technical skill. The story dictates the format, not the other way around.

02

Conversational AI design requires user empathy

The KFC chatbot worked technically — but making it feel human required thinking through what a user might type when confused, bored, or impatient. The entertainment features (quiz, jokes) came from asking "what happens in the gaps?" not just mapping the happy path.

03

Intent matching errors teach you how NLP really works

Dialogflow misinterpreting quiz answers and skipping responses forced a deep understanding of training phrases, entity assignment, and context management. Real debugging of NLP systems is the best education in how they work — and fail.

04

The strongest debate positions hold both sides

Researching 5 arguments for and 5 against eSports produced a more nuanced conclusion than taking a side. The most defensible position wasn't "eSports is good" or "bad" — it was "it depends on governance, context, and how you measure success."

05

Emerging technologies are defined by their trade-offs

Every experiment in this module — data visualisation, conversational AI, eSports — had genuine benefits and genuine risks. Emerging technologies aren't inherently good or bad; they're tools, and their impact depends entirely on how they're designed and governed.
